What are consetllations?

Constellations are an area on the celstial sphere that is a physically percievable pattern of stars. Constellations are the result of the human imagination. They are not real, but are a way to help us organize the night sky. They are usually a representation of an animal, person, or object. Origins of these constellations are quite old. The oldest known constellations are from the Babylonian period.

Modern constellations sets include 88 IAU accepeted constellations.

How to find constellations

To find constellations in the night sky you should find a dark location. Away from highly luminous aritifal light. An easy way to find constellations is by using north star polaris.

Then you can use a star map that will help you find the constellations you are looking using polaris as a reference in the night sky. I reccomend using a phone app that will help you find constellations in the night sky. Such as Stellerium or Sky Guide. They use GPS and will give accurate representation of the night sky at the exact second of the day. It also includes features for you to find objects using your phone's camera and position. Some constellations are seasonal. Meaning that they are only visible during certain times of the year. You should be able to identify them using the star map.

Objects in constellations for observation

Objects that you can find in constellations are of course stars. But there are also other objects that you can find in constellations. Such as: nebulas, galaxies, and star clusters.
